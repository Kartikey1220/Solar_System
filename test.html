Mars
Events
Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun, is a terrestrial world about half the size of Earth, with a diameter of 6,792 km. It orbits the Sun at an average distance of 228 million km, taking 687 Earth days for one year.
MOONS
Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos
ATMOSPHERE
Mars has a thin atmosphere, mostly carbon dioxide (95%), with traces of nitrogen and argon, exerting only 0.6% of Earth's atmospheric pressure. This leads to extreme cold, with average surface temperatures around -65°C (-85°F), ranging from -87°C at the poles to 20°C in equatorial summers. Dust storms can engulf the planet, and water exists as ice beneath the surface or in polar caps, but liquid water is unstable due to low pressure.
Exploration History
Human exploration began with flybys in the 1960s, like NASA's Mariner 4 in 1965, revealing a cratered, lifeless world. Landers such as Viking 1 (1976) conducted soil tests for life signs (none found). Rovers like Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, and Perseverance have mapped terrains, analyzed rocks, and searched for ancient microbial life. Orbiters like Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter provide high-resolution imagery.
