Venus Elongation

Alignment: Greatest elongation occurs when Venus, Earth, and the Sun align so that Venus appears farthest from the Sun in Earth’s sky — about 47° east or west of the Sun.
Appearance: Venus shines as a brilliant star‑like point, visible either just after sunset (Evening Star) or just before sunrise (Morning Star).
Visibility: This is the best time to observe Venus, since it is farthest from the Sun’s glare and highest above the horizon.
Frequency: Venus reaches greatest elongation roughly every 584 days, alternating between morning and evening appearances.
Angular Distance: The maximum separation is about 47°, much larger than Mercury’s (~28°), making Venus easier to spot
