Earth Inner layers

🌋 Mantle
The mantle lies beneath the crust and extends to about 2,900 km deep. It’s mostly solid rock but flows slowly, driving plate tectonics and volcanic activity. Temperatures range from 500°C to over 4,000°C.
🔥 Outer Core
This liquid layer of iron and nickel spans from 2,900 to 5,100 km deep. Its swirling motion generates Earth’s magnetic field, which protects the planet from solar radiation.
💎 Inner Core
At the center is a solid sphere of iron and nickel, about 1,220 km thick. Despite extreme heat, it stays solid due to immense pressure and may rotate slightly faster than the rest of Earth.
