Lunar Eclipse

Alignment: A lunar eclipse occurs when Earth passes directly between the Sun and the Moon, casting its shadow on the Moon.
Appearance: From Earth, the Moon appears darkened and often takes on a reddish or copper hue due to sunlight refracted through Earth's atmosphere.
Rarity: Total lunar eclipses happen roughly every 2 to 3 years, and they are visible from anywhere on Earth's night side when they occur.
